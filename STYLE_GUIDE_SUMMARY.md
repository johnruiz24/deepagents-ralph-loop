## THE UNMOVABLE RULES

These phrases and structures MUST appear (or close equivalents):

```
1. Opening: "Imagine [scenario]..."
2. Contrast: "Now, contrast this with..."
3. Question: "What if we could...?"
4. Shift: "It's a shift from X to Y"

5. Scope: "This article takes a different approach"
6. Belief: "We believe the true value of X lies not just in... but in..."
7. Journey: "That's why we'll take you on a complete journey..."

8. Production Gap: "This is where most tutorials end — and where the real engineering begins"

9. Conclusion Opening: "Throughout this article, we've moved beyond [theory]..."
10. Call to Action: "Your Move" (section title)
11. Final: "[Relevant quote]" — [Attribution]
```

## PROOF HIERARCHY YOU MUST USE

Every major claim follows this pattern:

1. **Assert** something about Ralph
2. **Show numbers** proving it works
3. **Translate to business value** ("That's the difference between...")
4. **Scale to enterprise** ("At scale, this means...")
5. **Explain why** using a principle or law

Example from Luis:
> Sequential: 120 sec | Parallel: 35 sec
> For common use case, that's difference between engaged user and abandoned assessment
> At enterprise scale: 33 hours → 9.7 hours → 3.4x more employees processed
> Explained by Amdahl's Law: speedup limited by sequential portions

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

- ❌ Opening without analogy (too technical)
- ❌ Writing "as you probably know..." (condescending)
- ❌ Code blocks longer than 20 lines
- ❌ Claims without numbers
- ❌ Architecture explanation without code
- ❌ Skipping enterprise scale consideration
- ❌ Conclusion that doesn't end with rhetorical question
- ❌ Generic best practices (must be Ralph-specific)
- ❌ Ending with "Thanks for reading!" (wrong tone)
- ❌ Missing "Your Move" section title
